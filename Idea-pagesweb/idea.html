THE VALUE OF IDEAS

Many people believe that ideas are worthless and only action and execution matter. This is a false belief. Ideas have real and tangible value. Ideas inspire action and direct the efforts of work. Not only do ideas have value, but their value has yet to be fully realized to humanity’s benefit. The real connections between ideas, actions, decisions, and opportunities elude society. Ideas are important because they are the building blocks of potential change. Ideas are possibilities. They are the units of adaptability and change that direct new thinking, grander goals, and more efficient action. We must understand ideas. We must value them. We must manage them.
